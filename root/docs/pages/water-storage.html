Water Storage

Clean water is essential for survival - learn how to store it safely

Why Store Water?

Water is the most critical element of emergency preparedness. A person can survive only about three days without water. During emergencies, your regular water supply may be cut off or contaminated. Having a reliable supply of clean water stored ensures your family's survival.

How Much Water to Store

Recommended Amount

One gallon per person per day

  • Half gallon for drinking
  • Half gallon for cooking and sanitation
  • More in hot climates or for nursing mothers
  • Don't forget water for pets

At minimum, store a two-week supply. A three-month supply is recommended when possible.

Storage Containers

Recommended Containers

  • Food-grade plastic containers
  • Glass containers
  • Fiberglass containers
  • Commercially bottled water
  • 55-gallon water barrels

Avoid These

  • Milk jugs (hard to clean, degrade quickly)
  • Previously used chemical containers
  • Non-food-grade plastics
  • Metal containers that may rust

How to Store Water

  1. Clean containers thoroughly - Wash with dish soap and rinse completely
  2. Sanitize - Use a solution of 1 teaspoon unscented bleach per quart of water, swish and rinse
  3. Fill with clean water - Use municipal tap water if available
  4. Add treatment if needed - If not using treated water, add 1/8 teaspoon bleach per gallon
  5. Seal tightly - Ensure containers are airtight
  6. Label and date - Mark containers with the fill date
  7. Store properly - Keep in a cool, dark place away from chemicals

Water Rotation

Replace stored water every 6-12 months to ensure freshness. Use the old water for plants, cleaning, or other household needs.

Emergency Water Purification

If you need to purify water during an emergency:

Boiling

  • Bring water to a rolling boil
  • Boil for at least 1 minute
  • 3 minutes at higher altitudes
  • Let cool before drinking

Chemical Treatment

  • Use unscented household bleach
  • 8 drops per gallon of clear water
  • 16 drops per gallon of cloudy water
  • Wait 30 minutes before drinking

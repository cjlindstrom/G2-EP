72-Hour Emergency Kit

Essential supplies to sustain your family for the first 72 hours of an emergency

Why 72 Hours?

In many emergency situations, it may take rescue workers up to 72 hours to reach affected areas. Having a well-stocked emergency kit ensures your family can be self-sufficient during this critical period.

Basic Supplies

Water

  • One gallon of water per person per day
  • Store a minimum of 3-day supply
  • Consider needs for pets
  • Water purification tablets or filter

Food

  • Non-perishable, easy-to-prepare items
  • Protein bars and granola bars
  • Dried fruit and nuts
  • Canned goods (with manual opener)
  • Crackers and peanut butter

First Aid

  • Adhesive bandages (various sizes)
  • Sterile gauze and adhesive tape
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Pain relievers
  • Prescription medications (2-week supply)
  • Medical equipment (glasses, hearing aids)

Tools & Safety

  • Flashlight with extra batteries
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Multi-tool or knife
  • Whistle (to signal for help)
  • Dust masks
  • Plastic sheeting and duct tape

Personal Items

  • Change of clothes for each family member
  • Sturdy shoes or boots
  • Rain gear
  • Warm blankets or sleeping bags
  • Personal hygiene items (toothbrush, soap, feminine supplies)
  • Baby supplies if needed (diapers, formula, bottles)
  • Pet supplies if applicable

Important Documents

Keep copies of important documents in a waterproof container:

  • Identification (driver's license, passport)
  • Insurance policies
  • Bank account information
  • Medical records and prescriptions
  • Emergency contact list
  • Recent photos of family members

Maintenance Tips

  • Check your kit every 6 months
  • Rotate food and water before expiration dates
  • Update documents and photos annually
  • Adjust kit contents as your family's needs change
  • Keep the kit in an accessible location known to all family members

Family Communication Plan

Stay connected with your family during emergencies

Why Have a Communication Plan?

During an emergency, your family may not be together. Having a communication plan ensures everyone knows how to reach each other and where to meet. Cell phone networks may be overloaded or unavailable, so having backup plans is essential.

Key Elements of Your Plan

Contact Information

  • All family members' phone numbers
  • Work and school phone numbers
  • Out-of-area emergency contact
  • Doctors and medical providers
  • Insurance companies

Meeting Places

  • Near your home (mailbox, tree)
  • Outside your neighborhood (school, church)
  • Outside your city (relative's home)

Out-of-Area Contact

Important Tip

Designate an out-of-area contact person. During local emergencies, it's often easier to make long-distance calls than local ones. Family members can check in with this person to relay their status.

  • Choose someone outside your immediate area
  • Make sure all family members have the contact information
  • Inform this person of their role
  • Practice using this system

Communication Methods

Primary Methods

  • Cell phone calls
  • Text messages (often work when calls don't)
  • Email
  • Social media check-ins

Backup Methods

  • Landline phones
  • Two-way radios
  • AM/FM radio for news
  • Written notes at meeting places

Tips for Communicating During Emergencies

  • Text first: Text messages require less bandwidth and are more likely to go through
  • Keep calls brief: If you can call, keep it short to free up the network
  • Use social media wisely: Mark yourself safe, but don't spread rumors
  • Conserve phone battery: Reduce screen brightness and close apps
  • Have backup power: Keep a portable charger in your emergency kit

Create Your Family Contact Card

Each family member should carry a contact card with:

  • Names and phone numbers of all family members
  • Out-of-area emergency contact
  • Meeting place addresses
  • Medical information and allergies
  • Insurance policy numbers

Laminate the cards and keep them in wallets, backpacks, and emergency kits.

Practice Your Plan

  • Review the plan with your family regularly
  • Practice evacuating to your meeting places
  • Test calling your out-of-area contact
  • Update contact information when it changes
  • Make it a habit - incorporate it into family home evening
